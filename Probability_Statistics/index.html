    Probability & Statistics Review¶

    This section is a focused refresher on the probability and statistics foundations you’ll use throughout biostatistics, clinical trials, machine learning, regression, and survival analysis.

    The goal is not to be overly theoretical—it's to build intuition so you can: - read formulas in papers confidently, - understand why methods work

    What you will learn¶

    By the end of this module, you will be able to:

    • Use the language of probability correctly: sample spaces, events, independence
    • Work comfortably with random variables (discrete and continuous)
    • Compute and interpret:
    • expectation, variance, covariance
    • conditional expectation
    • Recognize and use common distributions in biostatistics:
    • Bernoulli, Binomial, Poisson
    • Uniform, Normal, Exponential
    • Understand how distributions connect to real data problems:
    • counts, rates, measurement error, waiting times
    • Use core statistical summaries and functions:
    • mean/median/mode
    • variance/SD/IQR
    • correlation and covariance
    • Build a strong base for later topics:
    • regression (linear/logistic/Poisson)
    • survival analysis (hazard intuition)
    • Bayesian methods (priors/likelihood)
    • inference concepts (sampling variability)

    Who this module is for¶

    This section is ideal if you: - want a quick but clear refresh before starting regression/survival/ML, - feel shaky on distributions or expectation/variance

    You’ll get the most benefit if you already know basic algebra.

    Module roadmap¶

    This review is organized into five short chapters:

    1. Probability Basics
      Experiments, sample spaces, events, probability rules, and worked examples.

    2. Random Variables
      Discrete vs continuous random variables, PMF/PDF/CDF, and simulation intuition.

    3. Expectation & Variance
      Means, variances, standard deviation, and why these summaries matter in modeling.

    4. Probability Distributions
      Key distributions used constantly in biostatistics (Binomial/Poisson/Normal/etc.) and when to use them.

    5. Statistical Functions & Summaries
      Descriptive statistics, variability measures, correlation/covariance, and practical data summaries.
